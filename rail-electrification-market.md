<p><ul><li>DC System</li><li>AC System</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Rail electrification is categorized into DC and AC systems, each with distinct features and applications. DC systems typically operate at lower voltages, making them suitable for urban transit and shorter routes, as they provide quicker acceleration and easier maintenance. In contrast, AC systems, often used for long-distance and high-speed rail networks, operate at higher voltages, allowing for efficient power distribution over longer distances. The choice between these systems depends on infrastructure, operational efficiency, and regional requirements.</p></p>
