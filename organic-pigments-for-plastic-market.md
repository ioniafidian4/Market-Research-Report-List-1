<p><p>The organic pigments for the plastic market is primarily categorized into two types: azo pigments and non-azo pigments. Azo pigments are characterized by their vibrant colors and excellent stability, commonly used in various plastic applications due to their cost-effectiveness. Non-azo pigments, including phthalocyanines and quinacridones, offer broader color ranges and enhanced lightfastness, making them suitable for high-performance plastics. Both types cater to diverse industries, including packaging, automotive, and consumer goods, emphasizing sustainability and regulatory compliance.</p></p>
