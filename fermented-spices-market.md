<p><ul><li>Acetic Acid Bacteria</li><li>Lactic Acid Bacteria</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The fermented spices market can be categorized based on the dominant microorganisms involved in the fermentation process, primarily Acetic Acid Bacteria (AAB) and Lactic Acid Bacteria (LAB). AAB is crucial for producing vinegar-based ferments, enhancing flavor and preservation through acetic acid production. In contrast, LAB plays a vital role in the fermentation of various spices, contributing to flavor development, probiotic benefits, and increased shelf life by producing lactic acid. Both types enrich the culinary and health aspects of fermented spices.</p></p>
