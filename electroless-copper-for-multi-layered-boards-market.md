<p><p>Electroless copper is a crucial process in the manufacturing of multi-layered printed circuit boards (PCBs), providing reliable electrical connections. The market is segmented into horizontal and vertical electroless copper types. Horizontal electroless copper involves a process where substrates are positioned parallel to the solution surface, allowing uniform deposition across large areas. In contrast, vertical electroless copper employs a vertical orientation of substrates, optimizing space and promoting efficient deposition on complex geometries. Both methods enhance PCB performance and manufacturing efficiency.</p></p>
